# Workload and Learning Correlation Report (Synthetic)

## Overview

This synthetic report examines how weekly workload signals correlate with
certification study completion. All figures are fabricated and do not represent
any real organisation or individual.

## Insight: High Meeting Load

Employees with more than 20 meeting hours per week consistently show lower study
completion rates, regardless of role. This pattern holds even when total
available working hours are similar across employees.

## Insight: Focus Time Threshold

Optimal study completion appears when learners have between 12 and 18 meeting
hours per week and at least 15 focus hours per week. Below 12 focus hours per
week, learners report difficulty maintaining a consistent study schedule.

## Insight: Preferred Learning Slot

Learners who study during their stated preferred learning slot (morning,
afternoon, or evening) report higher adherence to planned study schedules than
learners assigned a generic default time.

## Recommendation

Scheduling agents should treat employees with more than 20 meeting hours per
week and fewer than 12 focus hours per week as capacity-constrained, and should
favour shorter, more frequent study sessions placed within the employee's
preferred learning slot.
